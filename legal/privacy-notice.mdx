## 9. Your data protection rights under CalOPPA

California Privacy Protection Act (CalOPPA) is the first state law in the United States to require commercial websites and online services to provide privacy notices. CalOPPA applies not only to California but to any person or company in the country (or possibly world) that operates websites and collects personally identifiable information from California consumers. These entities are required to post a visible privacy notice on their websites stating the information being collected and with whom it is being shared, and to adhere to this notice. For details, visit
https://consumercal.org/about-cfc/cfc-education-foundation/california-online-privacy- protection-act-caloppa-3/.
